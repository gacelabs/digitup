What is Etsy?

Etsy is the world's leading marketplace for handmade, vintage, and unique creative goods. With over 90 million active buyers, Etsy provides artists, crafters, and creative entrepreneurs with a ready-made audience looking for products they can't find in mainstream retail stores.

The platform supports physical products, digital downloads, craft supplies, and vintage items (at least 20 years old). From handmade jewelry and custom clothing to printable art and digital planners, Etsy's diverse marketplace caters to creative sellers of all types.

What Can You Sell?

Handmade Items: Products you make or design yourself – jewelry, clothing, art, home decor, bath products, and more. Etsy allows production partners for scaling.

Digital Products: Downloadable files like printable art, planners, patterns, invitations, and digital assets. Popular for passive income since there's no shipping or inventory.

Vintage Items: Items at least 20 years old. Popular categories include clothing, jewelry, collectibles, and home goods.

Craft Supplies: Materials and tools for other creators – beads, fabric, patterns, and specialty supplies.

Earning Potential

Etsy sellers range from hobbyists earning pocket money to full-time entrepreneurs making six figures. Typical part-time sellers earn $100-500/month, while dedicated sellers with optimized shops and marketing can earn $5,000-50,000+/month. Digital products offer the highest margins since there's no cost of goods after initial creation.

Pros

  • Massive built-in audience of 90M+ buyers
  • Trust and credibility of established marketplace
  • Easy shop setup – launch in hours
  • Digital products require no inventory
  • Seller tools for listings, shipping, and analytics
  • Global reach with localized shopping experiences

Cons

  • Transaction and payment processing fees (6.5%+ per sale)
  • Listing fees ($0.20 per listing)
  • High competition in popular categories
  • Algorithm changes can affect visibility
  • Must handle customer service and returns
